1923
The room · where you turn fifty-five

A stadium the age
of a legend.

Forest Hills Stadium was built in 1923, a horseshoe of weathered concrete crowned by eleven stone eagles, home of American tennis for half a century. Then the music came. On August 28, 1964, the Beatles arrived here by helicopter, set down on a grass tennis court while sixteen thousand people screamed. Two nights, thirty-one thousand fans, a record the venue has never broken.

You already know the helicopter part. Your crew has a name for exactly that entrance. #CopterSquad. Sixty-two years after the Fab Four came down out of the sky over these grounds, Fang Speare walks in on foot, through a quiet Tudor garden village, to turn fifty-five under an open sky. Ringo himself comes back to this same stage on October 1. The room keeps welcoming the ones who show up for the music.

The sky goes from gold to indigo over an open stage, the eagles turn to silhouettes, and just past the walls the Tudor rooftops go silent. Then the sub-bass starts, and the whole horseshoe becomes one body. what Saturday night feels like

The name · the myth · your own music

Fang Speare.

You did not pick your producer name by accident. It grew from a myth you traced yourself, from Japanese folklore back through Chinese legend, on a January afternoon of trading screenshots. Jiraiya means "Young Thunder," the toad-riding sage. And here is the part that was built for you: the woodblock master who printed Jiraiya's likeness in 1852, Utagawa Kunisada, is the same artist whose prints you collect and frame and give to the people you love. The floating world on your wall drew the sage whose name you now DJ under.

How it all relates · one current, two channels

One story runs the length of it. A Daoist immortal and his coin-spitting toad cross the sea to become Japan's toad hermit, who becomes the storm-calling folk hero Jiraiya, who gets carved into woodblocks by the very ukiyo-e masters you frame and gift (Kunisada, Kuniyoshi), who is reborn as Naruto's mentor-sage, the one who pours everything into the next generation and dies making sure they rise, who becomes you, riding the low end the way he rode the toad. Laid over that myth is a second channel, literary: the Shakespeare pun folded into your own name, and the Poe beat you cut with MC Lars, so the sage does not just drop, he speaks in verse. Folklore, woodblock, anime, and lit-hop, four rivers, one man on the bill.

Why this weekend is really a monument

Forty years,
in five stops.

The gift you gave in your twenties came back to you in your fifties. That is the whole story.

Fall 1985 · Phillips Exeter

Webster North, and the tone you set

The year you stood up to Sam Anson, the biggest bully in the dorm, and beat him. In Gordon's eyes you ended the bullying in Webster North and set the tone for everyone. That is who you have always been, the one who protects the room. Sophomore year, and the longest friendship in Gordon's life. It never stopped.

The 1990s · Foster City

You packed up your life

You dropped everything and moved west to help Gordon launch a career in an industry that barely existed yet. Roommates again. That act of loyalty is the root of the net-giver everyone leans on now. Then home to New York, for the family business and for art school.

2018 onward · Park Avenue

The open door

The spare bed always made. The shirt and tie lent before the big day. The one who hosts, funds, and shows up. Abundance, never scarcity. You bet on the people you love, out loud.

2022 · Ultra Miami, and every show since

Not a phase. A whole life.

Gordon took you to Ultra and you fell the way everyone does. Then came the years: EDC with the helicopter squad, the Bass Boat, Red Rocks, and more shows and more table-tennis rallies with Gordon and Brennan than anyone can count. The student became a devotee, then a regular, then a producer.

2026 · now

Fang Speare arrives

A modular rig, a DGX Spark, an AI record label, and a producer name pulled from a toad-riding sage. You did not follow Gordon down the AI rabbit hole. You sprinted ahead. Fifty-five, and the mentor became an artist.

A note from Gordon

Fos. Forty years ago you were across the hall in Webster North and I had no idea I had just met the person who would keep showing up for the rest of my life. You packed up your whole world and moved west so I could chase a job in an industry nobody believed in yet. I have never forgotten that. It is the reason I know what loyalty looks like.

So when I took you to Ultra, I was just trying to hand a little of it back. I did not expect you to run past me. Watching you become Fang Speare, an actual producer at fifty-five, debating the drop with me at midnight, gifting me a Kunisada off your own wall, has been one of the great joys of getting older next to you. And the way you have taken Brennan under your wing, backing him and cheering him on and blessing his every move, means more to me than I could ever put in a text.

This weekend is not a party. It is a thank-you. For the spare bed, the open door, the abundance, the forty years. Happy birthday, brother. Let's ride the low end.
