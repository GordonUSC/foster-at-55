The headliner

Closing · Main Stage

Tiësto

Breda, Netherlands · big-room / mainstage EDM · Grammy winner

The reason the night exists. Twenty-five years from trance pioneer to the biggest name in dance, and still the cleanest festival closer alive — "The Business," "Red Lights," "Adagio for Strings," the Ultra sets that pulled Foster into all of this in the first place. Expect the hits, the pyro-scale energy, and a skyline backdrop. Full-circle for the crew.

Death-metal kid turned techno force. Signature 150-BPM peak-time with melancholic melodies over electrifying basslines. Drumcode's "We Control" hit #1 on Beatport; her debut LP Bigger Than Techno just dropped this spring. Mixmag's "most exciting names in techno."

Dinner first

The pre-game · book ahead

Gage & Tollner

372 Fulton St, Downtown Brooklyn · oyster & chop house since 1879 · NYT Top 100

A landmarked Brooklyn institution — arched mirrors, a long dark-wood bar, gaslit glamour — closed in 2004 and lovingly revived in 2021 by chef Sohui Kim with Ben Schneider & St. John Frizell. Grass-fed dry-aged steaks, a raw bar of East Coast oysters, and a few dishes with a quiet Korean tilt. Exactly Foster's lane.

Order this
She-Crab Soup— the Edna Lewis classic, sherry-finished blue-crab bisque
Clams Casino— broiled with house kimchi + bacon butter, the Korean twist
Oysters Rockefeller— plus a raw-bar tower to start for the table
Dry-aged steaks & chops— the whole point of a chophouse
Parker House Rolls— + Devils-on-Horseback (dates, almond, bacon)
Baked Alaska— or the Brooklyn cheesecake, if there's room

🍸 Happy hour "teeny 'tinis" at the bar if you land early. Friday dinner service starts 5 PM.
